Samsung Galaxy S

The Samsung Galaxy S was released first in 2010 and was Samsungs first touchscreen phone. It was the first of Samsungs "S" line of phones. The Samsung Galaxy S release was announced by Samsung corporation in March 2010 and The Samsung Galaxy S was released later that year. The Samsung Galaxy S was not sold for long however due to a shortage in AMOLED displays. The Samsung Galaxy S is made in more than twelve different variations. When the Samsung Galaxy S was released it had the fastest processor in all the phone industry. The Samsung Galaxy S was also the thinnest touch screen phone at 9.9 milimeters, and the Samsung Galaxy S the first Samsung phone to be certified for DivX HD. As of 2013 over 25 million Samsung Galaxy S' were sold making it A hit for Samsung, Samsung corporation was so happy with the phone that the made many more Samsung phones in the Samsung S phone line. The Samsung Galaxy S features a 1 GHz ARM "hummingbird" processor the fastest processor in a mobile phone at that time, a PowerVR graphics processor, 2 or 4GB of internal flash memory, a 4 inch (1o centimeters) 480x800 pixel super AMOLED capacitive touchscreen display, Wi-fi connection, a 5-megapixel primary camera and a 0.3-megapixel secondary front-facing camera. The phone had a button at the bottom in the center that takes you to the home page of the phone. The phone had three buttons on the same side of the phone one turned the volume up and the other turned the volume down.
